BTC Thesis

Friday's 12-hour trend loss was the trigger. Doc resisted on the first drop ("how ugly the last bottom was") and the next close-below confirmed it. The "Hindsight Harry" admission: he should have hedged on last week's squeeze instead of waiting — the double trade above/below the trend was confirmation enough.

The trend-loss framework

This is the muscle-memory rule he repeats every stream:

  1. Range — the default first outcome
  2. Reversal — the second
  3. Reclaim & continuation — the third

Don't bet downside immediately on trend loss. Most plays chop first.

The inflection: 77.9K monthly value area low

Bull case: Reclaim monthly VAL → rotation higher into the 79s. That's the bonk zone for bears looking to short the relief.

Bear case: Fail back below previous month's values → grind down to 73–74K where yearly VWAP, previous quarterly VWAP, and previous monthly VWAP all sit on top of each other.

Playbook & Process

The recurring rules Doc repeats — pulled out of the Q&A segments.

Don't diddle in the middle Trade the extremities, not the mean. No-man's-land is negative EV. If you do trade the daily VWAP / mid-value, you get one strike — one trade. If it fails, you're done with that zone for the day.
Take profits early "It doesn't matter, take profits early." If you're slow-bleeding with a high win rate, the math is on the losses, not the exits. Pre-define your partials at levels you respect.
Time-based invalidation If your session-thesis squeeze doesn't materialize by session close, fully close. The fact that it plays out the next day isn't vindication — that's a different setup on a different day.
Start minimal, add one tool at a time Doc started with volume + daily VWAP + open interest. No order book, no footprints, no liquidity. Add complexity in isolation, learn its behavior, combine after. Trying to learn five tools at once = conflicting signals.
Magus is an exception — don't copy from day one Magus trades momentum and VWAP-area setups beginners shouldn't. He has 10 years of intraday discretion built up. You earn the right to take those trades; you don't start there.
Binance spot is the clean flow read right now Doc's been ignoring Coinbase on AGGR for ~2 months. Coinbase has been giving false absorption/exhaustion reads — net seller during uptrend, V-reverses. Not saying it's lying; saying it's not giving repeatable signals right now.
Patience is built through repeated mistakes Can't be taught. You have to click the wrong button enough times that future-you triggers the brake before the action. The discipline issue is "I need to be in this trade now" with no actual trigger.
