i.Where we are

Bitcoin lost its 12-hour trend on Friday around 79,000. By the time Doc opened the stream on Wednesday, daily VWAP was rejecting overhead. He didn't put on a full one-to-one hedge over the weekend, just a partial. He spent the first half hour of the stream explaining why.

The "Hindsight Harry" admission, his phrase: he should have hedged into the squeeze the prior week. The double trade above and below the trend was confirmation enough.

The levels in his head

79,000
Hedge-add zone. Any squeeze back here, the rest of the hedge goes on.
77,900
The inflection. Monthly value area low. Reclaim opens a rotation higher. Fail rolls the chart over.
73,500
The downside. Yearly VWAP, previous quarterly VWAP, previous monthly VWAP — all stacked on top of each other.

The framework he repeats every stream, drilled into his audience as muscle memory: range first, reversal second, reclaim and continuation third. Don't bet downside immediately on a trend loss — most plays chop before anything happens.

ii.Three trades

The session netted about three percent. Two clean wins and one scratch. Doc walked through each.

Trade One

The pre-open short

Bitcoin was up roughly one and a half percent in Asia, grinding into thick ask walls. A Trump-news candle expanded into the supply and fully retraced — a high-effort move into a high-resistance zone, absorbed and given back. Doc took the speculative short thirty minutes before New York opened. The rule he says out loud every time: pump into NY open, look for reversal. Dump into NY open, look for a pump.

He took profit near daily VWAP. The single-sided thirty-minute bleed in his favor never gave him a reason to take it earlier.

Result · +700 points · ~1%
Trade Two

Long the picobottom

He caught near the exact bottom tick. The target was a rotation back to weekly value area high and the monthly value area low at 77,900. The trade worked. But on the way up, the rounding action under the weekly value area high made him uneasy — he saw the possibility of a value-to-value rotation back to weekly VAL and didn't want to be sitting in a long that would force him to add into no-man's-land.

He cut at the weekly VAH. Self-critique on the stream: maybe he was overthinking the rounding. But the rule held. The elite entry gave him room to manage; he wasn't fighting the trade.

Result · +700 points · cut early
Trade Three

The re-add — effort absorbed at the lows

Shortly after he cut, a high-effort dump candle drove into the lows and immediately traded back through. The phrase: effort absorbed, take the reversal. He market-long right back into the candle that had broken down. Invalidation below weekly value area low, a clean line. Half off the first candle, held through the chop, sold the rest into the next push.

Result · +700 points · ~1%

iii.The spot plan

Doc cashed out near the highs in 2024 and 2025. He's been holding chips. His average on the BTC stack he's already rebuilding sits around 63 to 65 thousand, with a fresh tranche bought at 76,000 in May. The plan for the next week, in his words: spot-slam more Bitcoin, price-agnostic. He doesn't care about the exact entry. He wants to own the asset.

Last cycle Pepe was a hundred-X on paper. Capital-growth-wise it was closer to five hundred-X — because I sold spot on every trend loss and re-accumulated. Until it stopped working. Doc, on cycle math

The math he's running on Bitcoin: a 70K average to a 120K target is a conservative 2x. Hedging on trend losses and intraday trading around the position amplify the gain from there. Bitcoin won't give him a 65-70 percent Pepe-style retrace to compound off of. At most a 20 percent correction, and he probably catches 10 to 15 of that. Combined with intraday, "I think I'll be okay."

iv.The alts that wouldn't stop running

Three alt names Doc called out as the clean winners right now: VVV, Hyperliquid, and ZEC. He had a thesis on Hyperliquid above 43 ("probably all-time highs") and kept fading conviction longs from the community. They ran and he missed them. The lesson he was processing live, more than the trade:

If you really wanted a HYPE stack and you faded it the whole way — just say fuck it. Buy thirty-five of the hundred coins you wanted, here. If it rolls over to thirty, buy the other sixty-five. Now you have a position.

Doc, on positioning when you're behind

The broader take — pivoting to Solana not because Solana specifically matters but because it's the cleanest example of the pattern he sees across the alt board:

Like we did have FTX last cycle, right? You look at Bitcoin — Bitcoin had FTX and went lower. It's very hard for me to look at this and go: we're not in an accumulation range already. The fact that Bitcoin already drove up this high is not something I was expecting. Doc, on the alt board

The structural read: the market is "not as bearish as it once seemed to me." A 38-percent bounce in Bitcoin from the lows came in higher than he initially expected. So dip toes back in. Get some allocation in the coins you like. Knowing it can still go lower. The discomfort of I'm not buying the lows — that's probably the right discomfort. The people who bought VVV and HYPE and ZEC just got tired of waiting. "They don't care anymore. They just want exposure."

v.Why this bear may be different

The longest segment of the educational portion was structural. Doc has been thinking about why this drawdown feels different from the last one, and he laid out the comparison directly.

  • The FUD
    Crypto-native, weekly. Luna and UST peg, 3AC, Celsius, BlockFi, FTX, on-chain liquidation hunts, DeFi hacks, the rehypothecation cascade. Every week another traumatizing event. Zero crypto-native FUD. The only macro event was the Israel-Iran headline candle — and Bitcoin had already washed out and went up on the news.
  • The driver
    Leverage unwinds and cascading insolvencies. Coins rehypothecated across multiple lenders. OG whales — fifty-thousand and hundred-thousand BTC dormant wallets — distributing into Saylor as the price-agnostic bidder. Saylor ran out of dry powder. Leverage longs caught.
  • The drawdown shape
    Multi-month accumulation range after the deleveraging event. Then bull market. Drawdown z-score matches the severity of the 3AC and Luna events. Plausibly the same template — multi-month range, then up.
  • The bidders
    Crypto natives and early ETFs. Institutional money. "Boomer" bidders looking at a multi-month consolidation at a clean support-resistance flip and saying: that looks like a place to bid. Bitcoin is institutional now.
Could this be the easiest bear market of all time? Time will tell — way too early to say. But the longer we go sideways, and the more we approach the prophesied four-year cycle bottom, the more it becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy once again. Doc, on cycle structure

vi.The rules he keeps repeating

The Q&A segments are where the playbook surfaces. Pulled from the stream, the rules Doc repeats most often — and what each is for.

  1. Don't diddle in the middle. Trade the extremities, not the mean. No-man's-land is negative EV. If you must trade the daily VWAP or mid-value: one strike — one trade. If it fails, you're done with that zone for the day.
  2. Take profits early. "It doesn't matter, take profits early." If you're slow-bleeding with a high win rate, the math is on the losses, not the exits. Pre-define your partials at levels you respect.
  3. Time-based invalidation. If your session-thesis squeeze doesn't materialize by session close: fully close. The fact that it plays out next day is not vindication — that's a different setup on a different day.
  4. Start minimal. Add one tool at a time. Doc started with volume, daily VWAP, and open interest — nothing else. Add complexity in isolation, learn its behavior, combine after. Five tools at once is five conflicting signals.
  5. Magus is the exception. Don't copy him from day one. Magus trades momentum and VWAP-area setups beginners shouldn't. He has ten years of intraday discretion built up. You earn the right to those trades, you don't start there.
  6. Binance spot is the clean read right now. Doc has been ignoring Coinbase on AGGR for roughly two months. Coinbase has been giving false absorption and exhaustion reads — net seller during the uptrend, V-reverses. Not lying, he's careful to say. Just not giving repeatable trade signals right now.
  7. Patience can't be taught — it has to be felt. You have to click the wrong button enough times that future-you triggers the brake before the action. The discipline issue is always the same: I need to be in this trade right now, with no actual trigger.
