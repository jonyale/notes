The decision on the table

Advertisers aren't coming. Do we switch to paid?

Short version: don't frame it as "free vs paid." Fix two leaks, then pick a rail. Three things are capping monetization right now —

  1. Too small for advertisers (~3,900 reach) — and ~40% of every signup cohort never makes it onto the list at all.
  2. Growth is single-channel and front-loaded — 64% of the entire list arrived in the launch quarter, almost all via Twitter/X. Referrals are switched off.
  3. You already have a paid product (The Paragon) and barely point at it — its link gets 3–5 clicks an entire edition.

The two questions, answered

Q1 — Why aren't advertisers biting?
It's a SIZE problem

Not engagement, not positioning. A sponsor buying you today gets ~250–320 clicks and a line item worth roughly $120–175 per send. That's below the floor (~10k–20k subscribers) most direct crypto sponsors and ad networks screen for.

Your engagement would earn a premium CPM — there just isn't enough volume underneath it to matter to a buyer yet.

Q2 — Is the list good enough for paid?
Yes — the core is real

~1,900–2,100 weekly openers and ~250–320 weekly clickers, with churn that's low and improving (3–9% lifetime). People who confirm, stay. That's the lifetime value a paid motion needs.

The hook is the trader voices + the proprietary "built-with-Claude" tooling — content readers can't get free on X.

The most important nuance: the click rate is mostly chart taps

⚠ Read before quoting the click rate to anyone

~90% of all clicks are people enlarging a chart, not clicking through to a destination.

Across three editions inspected at the link level, chart-image expands were 91% / 93% / 91% of every email click. Charts are clickable images that open the full-size PNG in a new tab.

The clean proof: the one recent edition with no clickable charts ("Defense so I can play offense," May 18) saw its click rate collapse to ~1% — that's CookBook's true outbound click rate once chart taps are stripped out.

So: the ~12% headline CTR is a real engagement / attentiveness signal (people study the charts) but must not be sold to advertisers as click-through intent. A sponsor's link should expect ~1–2%, which is normal, not elite.

The biggest, cheapest fix: the signup leak

🚨 2,509 people wanted in and never made it onto the list

That's people who hit subscribe but never confirmed the double-opt-in email — so they receive nothing. It holds in every cohort (35–41%) and is slightly worsening, so it's structural, not a launch artifact.

Counting everyone who ever signed up, the list should be ~6,450 emailable. Killing or fixing double-opt-in (and re-confirming the ~520 fresh pendings) is the single highest-ROI move — it directly grows the one number that gates both ads and paid.

Per-trader: the honest answer is "we can't tell yet"

Why a per-trader leaderboard would mislead

We wanted to rank the five voices by attention to find the paid hook. The data doesn't currently support it: beehiiv tracks only chart-image clicks and a few nav links — there are no per-trader section links or tags. Chart clicks are the only per-section signal, and they're biased three ways:

  • Position — whoever's first (Magus) gets the most clicks regardless of content.
  • Chart count — Stoic and Mercury run few or no charts by design, so they can't accumulate clicks.
  • No read signal — a great section with no chart scores zero.

The one signal that survives the bias check: Charlie's alt-coin charts pull the most clicks per chart despite appearing late in the email — his content travels. Beyond that, "who's the hook" is currently unmeasured, not measured.

The fix (costs nothing): add one tagged link per trader section going forward (e.g. utm_content=magus|doc|charlie|stoic|mercury), and tag the Twitter subscribe links per account. After ~4–6 tagged editions we can rank voices cleanly and finally prove which account drives the 79%.

Recommendations, in priority order

  1. Kill or fix double opt-inDo now
    ~40% of every cohort never confirms. A settings change (plus re-confirming the ~520 fresh pendings) grows emailable reach immediately — the number that gates both ads and paid.
  2. Turn on the referral programDo now
    Free, on-brand growth. A trader audience shares charts readily, and it reduces the all-eggs-in-Twitter risk.
  3. Instrument links: per-trader + per-accountNext edition
    UTM-tag each trader's section and each account's subscribe link. Turns "who's the hook" and "who drives acquisition" from guesses into data.
  4. Monetize via Paragon first — not a new paywallThis quarter
    You already have the paid product and elite engagement; you're just barely pointing at it. Move the upsell out of the P.S. into a real placement and measure newsletter → Paragon conversion before building separate CookBook tiers.
  5. Defer direct ad sales until ~8–10k emailableLater
    The engagement will command premium CPMs then. Today the absolute volume is too low to attract or justify a sponsor relationship.
  6. Always make charts clickable — and never sell chart-taps as ad CTRStanding rule
    The May 18 collapse shows clickable charts drive the engagement signal; just price ads on real outbound behavior (~1–2%), not the headline number.

Methodology & caveats

Pulled live from the beehiiv API/MCP on 2026-06-23: publication aggregates (90-day + all-time), the 15 most-recent editions with full email/web stats, link-level click breakdowns on 4 editions, and subscriber counts by status and acquisition quarter (which reconcile exactly to 3,944 active / 332 inactive / 2,509 pending).

  • Open rates are inflated by Apple Mail Privacy Protection — directional only; we leaned on clicks/CTOR.
  • "Click rate" is ~90% chart-image expands; true outbound click intent is ~1–2%.
  • Per-trader attribution is not yet possible (no section-level links) — see the fix above.
  • Paid-tier config couldn't be read directly (API token lacks billing scope); all other signals confirm free-only.
